# Introduction

AI generated content WILL hurt your brand.

It doesn't matter whether we're talking about social posts, emails to clients, or blog content. More and more people are becoming aware of what AI content looks like, and when they see it, it lowers their estimation of you and your service. 

The problem is that AI generated content exhibits certain patterns, word choices and structural elements that can be identified by both human readers and AI detection tools.

This research document explores what makes content look so obviously “AI” and provides some strategies to avoid detection.

# 👀 What Makes Content Look AI-Generated

## 1. Overused Vocabulary

AI models like ChatGPT tend to favour certain words and phrases that appear with high frequency in their training data.

These words often stand out to human readers and AI detection tools as indicators of “machine generated” content.

Some of the most commonly overused words and phrases in AI-generated content include:

- **Abstract concepts**: "Delve," "realm," "landscape," "journey," "tapestry"
- **Transition phrases**: "Moreover," "furthermore," "additionally," "however"
- **Intensifiers**: "Significantly," "substantially," "considerably," "notably"
- **Formal constructions**: "It is worth noting that," "It is important to consider," "As previously mentioned"
- **Generic expressions**: "In today's fast-paced world," "A deeper understanding," "Unlock potential"

## 2. Predictable Content Structure

AI-generated content often follows highly predictable structural patterns:

- **Rigid introduction-body-conclusion format**: Even for casual content, AI tends to use academic structures
- **Formulaic paragraph length**: Similar paragraph lengths throughout a piece
- **Balanced sections**: Unnaturally even distribution of content across sections
- **Excessive signposting**: Overuse of phrases like "In this article," "Let's explore," or "Let's dive into"
- **Rule of three pattern**: AI frequently organises information in groups of three items or points

## 3. Sentence Construction Patterns

AI models exhibit recognisable patterns in how they construct sentences:

- **Repetitive sentence structures**: Overreliance on similar sentence constructions
- **Lack of natural variation**: Human writers naturally vary their sentence structures and lengths
- **Predictable transitions**: "But here's the thing," "It's not just X, it's also Y," "The result? More people are..."
- **Question-answer format**: Frequently posing rhetorical questions and immediately answering them
- **Consistent formality level**: Maintaining the same level of formality throughout, unlike human writing which naturally varies

## 4. Lack of Authentic Voice

AI-generated content often lacks the authentic qualities that make human writing distinctive:

- **Generic perspectives**: Presenting balanced viewpoints without strong opinions
- **Detached metaphors**: Using metaphors that feel researched rather than lived
- **Vague references to experiences**: "That soft morning light" instead of specific, detailed experiences
- **Absence of idiosyncrasies**: Missing the quirks, imperfections, and unique voice of human writers
- **Overly polished tone**: Lacking the natural roughness and imperfections of human writing

# 🔎 How AI Detectors Work

It’s important to understand that AI detectors are easily accessible and being deployed in a variety of different places!

The best way to guard from being detected is to understand what they are looking for!

## 1. Statistical Analysis

AI detectors analyse text for statistical patterns that differentiate between human and AI-generated content:

- **Token probability analysis**: Examining how predictable each word choice is in sequence
- **Perplexity measurement**: Calculating how surprised a model would be by each next word
- **Burstiness evaluation**: Analysing the variation in word and sentence complexity
- **Entropy assessment**: Measuring the randomness and unpredictability of the text

## 2. Linguistic Pattern Recognition

Detectors look for specific linguistic patterns common in AI-generated text:

- **Phrase frequency analysis**: Identifying overused phrases and constructions
- **Sentence structure examination**: Looking for repetitive or predictable sentence patterns
- **Transition word analysis**: Detecting formal or academic transition patterns
- **Stylometric features**: Analysing the unique "fingerprint" of writing style

## 3. Machine Learning Classification

Modern AI detectors use machine learning to improve detection accuracy:

- **Training on labeled datasets**: Learning from examples of known human and AI text
- **Feature extraction**: Identifying subtle patterns that humans might miss
- **Confidence scoring**: Providing probability estimates rather than binary judgments
- **Adaptive learning**: Continuously improving as new AI generation techniques emerge

## 4. Limitations of AI Detectors

Despite their sophistication, AI detectors have several limitations:

- **False positives**: Sometimes flagging human-written content as AI-generated
- **Difficulty with hybrid content**: Struggling to identify content that combines AI and human writing
- **Challenges with technical content**: Often misclassifying specialised or technical writing
- **Vulnerability to deliberate evasion**: Can be fooled by techniques specifically designed to avoid detection

# 📝 Alternative Content Structures to Appear More Human

AI-written content often follows set patterns. Here are different ways to make content sound more naturally human-written:

## 1. Mix Up Paragraph Lengths

- **Short and long paragraphs**: Add some one-sentence paragraphs for emphasis
- **Uneven sections**: Make some parts longer or shorter than others on purpose
- **Break normal formats**: Sometimes start with a story instead of an introduction

## 2. Natural Flow Between Ideas

- **Fewer obvious transitions**: Remove some transitions completely for a more natural flow
- **Talk like people talk**: Replace formal transitions with casual phrases like "Here's the thing" or "I've noticed that"
- **Smooth topic changes**: Change topics without announcing it

## 3. Break the Rule of Three

- **Use different list lengths**: Make lists with 2, 4, or 5 items instead of always 3
- **Uneven support for points**: Use 1 strong point for some ideas and several for others
- **Break patterns on purpose**: If you notice a pattern forming, break it

## 4. Add Personal Touch

- **Share real stories**: Add specific, detailed personal experiences
- **Use "I" naturally**: Include "I" statements that show real opinions
- **Include some flaws**: Add occasional uncertainty or admitted mistakes

## 5. Mix Up Structure

- **Change sentence types**: Mix simple, compound, and complex sentences
- **Use fragments sometimes**: Include incomplete sentences for emphasis
- **Repeat for effect**: Sometimes repeat words or phrases in a way that feels natural

## 6. Real References

- **Use specific examples**: Replace vague references with clear details
- **Ground comparisons in reality**: Create comparisons based on everyday experiences
- **Give context for references**: Don't just name-drop; show understanding

## 7. End Naturally

- **Don't sum up everything**: End with a new thought or implication
- **Use soft endings**: Sometimes end without clearly signaling it's the end
- **Ask real questions**: End with questions that don't have obvious answers

# 👥 Effective Prompting Techniques to Avoid AI Detection

How you instruct AI tools like ChatGPT can greatly reduce the chance that the resulting content will be spotted as AI-generated:

## 1. Ask for Simple, Plain Language

- **Request everyday words**: Ask for common words instead of fancy or academic language
- **Specify simple sentences**: Request short, clear sentences that a child could understand
- **Ask for fourth-grade level**: Specify writing at a basic reading level that sounds natural

## 2. Request Informality

- **Ask for casual tone**: Request writing that sounds like a friend talking
- **Specify contractions**: Ask to use contractions (don't, can't, we're) like people do in conversation
- **Request slang or common phrases**: Ask for everyday expressions people actually use

## 3. Ask for Personal Stories

- **Request anecdotes**: Give the AI a story (or access to a repo of stories) that it can incorporate —> make sure to mention it should not lie!
- **Specify first-person view**: Request content written from a personal perspective
- **Ask for opinion statements**: Request clear stances on topics instead of balanced views (preferably tell it your opinion prior)

## 4. Request Uneven Structure

- **Ask for varied paragraph length**: Request a mix of very short and longer paragraphs
- **Specify pattern breaks**: Ask to avoid consistent patterns in how information is presented
- **Request natural flow**: Ask for writing that doesn't follow academic structure

## 5. Ask for Simplification

- **Request examples over abstractions**: Ask for concrete examples rather than theoretical concepts
- **Specify direct explanations**: Ask to avoid roundabout ways of explaining things
- **Request everyday comparisons**: Ask for comparisons to common experiences

## 6. Ask for Casual Transitions

- **Request natural bridges**: Ask for casual ways to move between ideas
- **Specify reduced signposting**: Request fewer formal transition phrases
- **Ask for thought jumps**: Request occasional less-connected ideas like humans naturally have

## 7. Request Imperfections

- **Ask for hesitations**: Request occasional uncertainties or hedging
- **Specify some repetition**: Ask for natural word repetition rather than perfect synonym use
- **Request thought corrections**: Ask for occasional self-corrections or clarifications

## 8. Ask for Strong Opinions

- **Request clear stances**: Ask for definite positions (YOUR POSITIONS) rather than balanced views
- **Specify emotional language**: Request content that shows feelings about the topic
- **Ask for certainty**: Request confident statements rather than overly cautious ones

## 9. Request Concrete Details

- **Ask for specifics**: Request precise details rather than general descriptions
- **Specify numbers and data**: Ask for specific figures when relevant
- **Request sensory details**: Ask for descriptions that involve sights, sounds, or feelings

## 10. Request Natural Variety

- **Ask for mixed formality**: Request some parts to be more formal, others more casual
- **Specify tone shifts**: Ask for changes in tone throughout the piece
- **Request conversational elements**: Ask for questions, expressions of surprise, or direct address

# 🤖 Common Mistakes in Prompting

Avoiding these common mistakes when prompting AI can help produce more natural, human-like content:

## 1. Too-Broad Instructions

- **Problem**: Vague instructions like "Write about climate change" lead to generic, detectable content
- **Solution**: Use specific, detailed instructions that narrow the focus and provide context

## 2. Not Specifying Tone

- **Problem**: Without style guidance, AI defaults to a formal, academic tone
- **Solution**: Clearly request a specific tone (casual, friendly, etc.)

## 3. Not Giving Background

- **Problem**: Instructions without context lead to generic, disconnected writing
- **Solution**: Include audience info, purpose and relevant background

## 4. Ignoring Format Needs

- **Problem**: Not specifying format results in predictable structures
- **Solution**: Request specific formatting that breaks from standard templates

## 5. Not Directing Voice

- **Problem**: Without direction, AI uses a neutral, balanced voice
- **Solution**: Specify viewpoint, biases and opinions to include

## 6. Asking for Perfect Grammar

- **Problem**: Perfect grammar can be a clear sign of AI generation
- **Solution**: Allow for occasional casual elements or conversational style

## 7. Not Reviewing and Changing

- **Problem**: Using AI output without human editing makes detection more likely
- **Solution**: Always review, change, and add your personal touch to AI-generated content

# 🤔 Practical Implementation Strategies

Here are practical ways to implement the techniques discussed in this research:

### 1. Two-Step Prompting Process

1. **Initial generation**: Use AI to create a basic draft
2. **Refinement prompt**: Follow up with specific instructions to humanise the content

### 2. Hybrid Content Creation

- **AI scaffolding**: Use AI to create an outline or structure
- **Human filling**: Add personal examples, opinions, and unique insights
- **AI polishing**: Use AI to help with editing while maintaining the human elements

### 3. Persona-Based Prompting

- Create a detailed persona with specific traits, experiences, and writing style
- Request that the AI write consistently from this persona's perspective
- Include background details that inform the persona's viewpoint

### 4. Template Modification

- Start with AI-generated content
- Deliberately break predictable patterns by:
    - Rearranging sections
    - Varying paragraph lengths
    - Adding personal asides or tangents
    - Introducing stylistic inconsistencies

### 5. Content Transformation Tools

Several tools can help transform AI-generated content to appear more human:

- **Humanising tools**: Services like Undetectable AI, HIX Bypass and AIHumaniser
- **Style transfer applications**: Tools that can apply specific writing styles to content
- **Readability adjusters**: Applications that can vary complexity levels throughout a text
